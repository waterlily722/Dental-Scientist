Dental segmentation template.

This template is a compact low-resource research sandbox for benchmarked dental segmentation.

Focus on ideas that are clinically meaningful, easy to execute, and easy to compare against the baseline.

Recommended idea families:
- improving boundary quality and overlap metrics
- radiograph-aware preprocessing for cleaner masks
- imbalance-aware region losses
- uncertainty estimation near ambiguous boundaries
- lightweight segmentation trade-offs under fixed data and splits

Avoid or treat as high-risk:
- any change that alters benchmark fairness or split integrity
- using external datasets, pseudo-labels, or external foundation models
- label-breaking augmentations or unrealistic geometry
- ideas that require major rewrites of the segmentation stack
- ideas that depend on large compute budgets

A strong contribution is not only a better overlap score. It can also be cleaner boundaries, better performance on small structures, or a clearer study of what improves dental mask quality and why.
