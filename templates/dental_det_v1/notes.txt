Dental detection template.

This template is a compact low-resource research sandbox for benchmarked dental detection.

Focus on ideas that are clinically meaningful, easy to execute, and easy to compare against the baseline.

Recommended idea families:
- improving small-lesion recall
- radiograph-aware preprocessing for localization
- imbalance-aware detection losses or sampling
- confidence calibration and false-positive control
- lightweight detector trade-offs under fixed data and splits

Avoid or treat as high-risk:
- any change that alters benchmark fairness or split integrity
- using external datasets, pseudo-labels, or external foundation models
- label-breaking augmentations or unrealistic geometry
- ideas that require major rewrites of the detection stack
- ideas that depend on large compute budgets

A strong contribution is not only a better mAP. It can also be stronger recall on hard findings, fewer false positives, or a clearer study of what improves dental lesion localization and why.
