Dental keypoint template.

This template is a compact low-resource research sandbox for benchmarked dental keypoint and landmark estimation.

Focus on ideas that are clinically meaningful, easy to execute, and easy to compare against the baseline.

Recommended idea families:
- improving landmark precision and stability
- geometry-aware losses or decoding
- radiograph-aware preprocessing for landmark visibility
- uncertainty estimation for ambiguous tooth landmarks
- lightweight keypoint model trade-offs under fixed data and splits

Avoid or treat as high-risk:
- any change that alters benchmark fairness or split integrity
- using external datasets, pseudo-labels, or external foundation models
- label-breaking augmentations or unrealistic geometry
- ideas that require major rewrites of the keypoint stack
- ideas that depend on large compute budgets

A strong contribution is not only a lower localization error. It can also be more stable landmarking on hard teeth, better geometric consistency, or a clearer study of what improves dental keypoint estimation and why.
